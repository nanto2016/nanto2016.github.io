Game Description

This year's game involves taking donut-shaped discs (called GPs) and placing them in “stations” to earn points.

There are two teams: blue and yellow, each made up of two robots.

The GPs are located in dispensers that the robots can access. Each station is unique in how it awards points and how it can be “controlled.”

Controlling the Stations

Each station can be controlled by a team based on the GPs placed there. Controlling stations can give a team a significant advantage, but strategy is essential.

The playing field is made up of 9 stations arranged in a tic-tac-toe grid. Controlling 3 stations in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal line multiplies the points earned from those stations for the controlling team.

To better understand the "TakTik Toe Multipliers," check out the interactive explanation of the game.

There are 4 types of stations:

  • The Post;
  • The Inverted Post;
  • The Horizontal Post;
  • And the Curling Area.

For more information about the different types of stations, refer to the game's interactive explanation.

Unfortunately, the interactive explanation does not cover the curling area, which is the most important station in the game.

Curling Area

The Curling Area is the most unique, challenging, and rewarding station.

It consists of 4 concentric zones and a pole in the center. Robots are not allowed to enter this area.

PJs thrown into one of the zones are worth a specific number of points based on the zone: the closer to the center, the more points. The first GP thrown onto the central pole is worth 500 POINTS!!! Each subsequent GP is worth fewer points: 400, 300, 200… down to 0.

The curling area is controlled by the team with the most GPs in the closest zone that contains at least one GP. For example, if the yellow team has 5 GPs in the 10-point zone and the blue team has 1 GP on the central pole, the blue team controls the curling area. If there is a tie, no team controls it.
