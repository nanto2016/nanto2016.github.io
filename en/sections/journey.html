The journey of the Creative Industry

in a comic book

September

The team gathers, anticipating the start of the competition.

"The last two years have been tough, but we're ready to overcome the challenges this year."

October

Little by little, mysterious images are revealed by the CRC. The team analyses them without success.

"Now that the game is revealed, I look back at the top-secret Tuesdays, and I don't really see any resemblance with the game... Guess we got tricked!"

Kickoff

All participants were eagerly waiting for this moment to jump into action!

"I think my second favorite moment at the kickoff, after the explanation of the rules, was when we bought a pair of CRC socks. Awesome."

November

The work continues! Meetings, design, planning, construction...

"Nothing's more fun than throwing out a ton of ideas just after learning how the game of the year will work!"

Website: The idea of an interactive explanation of TakTik 2025 is proposed.

Kiosk: We're searching for a theme... what will it be?

Programming: The team decides not to participate in the preliminary challenges due to a lack of members.

Video: It is agreed upon that a tutorial could easily be made, once the robot is built, showcasing one of its components.

December

A theme is finally chosen for the team: "The Creative Industry"

"It's surprising that such a large school has so few resources for robotics... and on top of that, a good part of the team barely has time to get involved."

Website: The interactive explanation proposed in November enters development. It is created using Godot.

Kiosk: A character creation activity that would be put in the kiosk is considered.

Video: Given that the idea of a "robotics weekend" has been suggested, the team plans to shoot the tutorial and the eventual video during that weekend.

Holidays

The component that made the most progress during the holidays is the website: nearly the entire site you are currently reading was created over these two weeks!

january

Upon returning from the holidays, the team sees the competition approaching and doubles down on their efforts!

"We can't afford, for the third year in a row, to arrive at the competition without a robot. We need to get a working robot as quickly as possible."

Website: Unfortunately, the preliminary deadlines are approaching, and the journey of the Industry cannot be documented further. The web team focuses on finalizing the writing and design.

Kiosk: The instructions are clear... no kiosk without a robot. The Industry dedicates all its resources to the robot.

Video: The same goes for the video team. The finance department, seeing the urgency of the situation, has ordered HR to transfer all employees to mechanics and programming!
